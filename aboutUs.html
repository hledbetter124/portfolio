Why We Exist

In the process of researching this topic I realized the tests described in my sources sounded really familiar so I asked my Mom if I had an IEP (Individualized Education Plan, basically a long document summing up all your good and bad qualities in a classroom) and turns out not only did I have one, I actually had two— one from when I was nine years old, and one from when I was a senior in high school. To fully disclose my personal connection to this issue I’ll share what was in those IEPs, and this article will explain the disparities between them. The one from when I was 9 actually didn’t say I have ADHD but instead diagnosed me with low grade Autism (called Asbergers at the time), this IEP also said I had a GAI (called IQ at the time) of 139. Fast forward to senior year, I had never been medicated and felt like I definitely had ADHD so I made my own appointment to be assessed again. This time they did diagnose me with ADHD, said I’m on the boarder line for an ASD (Autism Spectrum Disorder), and gave me a GAI of 137 (I guess 9 year old me was smarter). The fact that I fit this category, and have had difficulties in the classroom for my whole life solidified my interest in advocating for our Twice-Exceptional students of the next generation so hopefully they don’t have to feel stressed and confused about their “potential” like I was.
