What Needs To Happen

Linked Article

So far we’ve defined what a twice-exceptional student is, why they need a different learning experience, what happens to them in schools now and why none of the options are ideal as well as how past discourse on this topic has misdirected and ultimately ineffective. Now it’s time to talk about what we can do. I think two courses of action need to be taken simultaneously. The first is to offer assistance in the short term- as explained in the Mystery Misdiagnosis article it’s pretty easy to identify twice-exceptional students and Cornoldi’s study showed twice-exceptional students can learn in a normal classroom just without the busywork. These two factors combine to make it relatively simple for our current classrooms to adapt to twice exceptional students. First, teachers would need to be trained on the characteristics displayed by twice-exceptional students, this can be done pretty easily through something as simple as a YouTube video. Second, teachers need to have discretion and even be encouraged to forgive certain students homework if they understand the concepts and do well on tests. These two simple steps could relieve a lot of anxiety for a lot of twice-exceptional students. However, this isn’t ideal, the twice-exceptional students are still going to be bored in class and not have their brains as active as they could be which is where my long term solution comes into play This one is admittedly pretty optimistic but if higher education institutions start adding twice-exceptional students to their education curricula learning consultants can come out of school being prepared to deal with them. A study recently published looking at a school that had implemented a specific program designed to teach twice-exceptional students showed that given the right support twice-exceptional students can achieve at the highest level (Speirs-Neumeister, 2024). This figure shows the support systems the school had implemented and you can see that besides for a few small things all of those services are available at every public school in America for either Gifted or Challenged students, they just have to be combined for twice-exceptional students.
