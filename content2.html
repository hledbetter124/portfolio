The Three Paths of Today's Twice-Exceptional Student

Placed in Special-Ed

This is generally the rarest path and can even be beneficial in taking pressure off of a student but they teach the student they don’t have the potential they do and they’ll usually end up under achieving (Arky, 2023). This can also have long term consequences on a student’s mental health as knowing you’re smart in some weird way while told you’re disabled can be damaging (Arky, 2023).

Placed in Gifted Program

It’s unclear if more twice-exceptional students end up in gifted programs or just in normal classrooms but it does seem this is much more common than being placed in a special-ed program for twice-exceptional students. This path doesn’t have the immediate underachievement as the former but does have some of its own consequences. Firstly, twice-exceptional students in gifted programs have a tendency to get labeled as lazy because all their teachers know about them is their IQ score (Arky, 2023). This has resulted in a well documented phenomenon known as “Gifted Kid Burnout Syndrome.” Gifted kid burnout has been coined by the internet after hundreds of Gen-Z students who had been placed in gifted programs at some time in their early education were now underachieving in school due to not having learned any study skills in primary school (Arky, 2023). Because of this, twice-exceptional students who are placed in a gifted program have a much higher likelihood of being diagnosed with anxiety and depression later in life (Arky, 2023).

Remain in Normal Program

This also might be the most common path for twice-exceptional students but it remains unclear, this path doesn’t necessarily have unintended consequences, more like knowledgeable concessions. According to a study based in Italy in 2022 and 2023 discovered twice-exceptional students who remain in the classroom were likely to be either at or just below average for grades with test scores in the top percentiles of their peers (Cornoldi et. al., 2023). This displays how twice-exceptional students don’t struggle with the content in their classes, in fact it’s just the opposite, they excel at understanding concepts but struggle to get busy work done when they already know what they’re doing.
